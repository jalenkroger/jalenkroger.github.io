The Growing Gap in Rural Mental Health Care

By: Rayna Pytlik

Scroll to Explore

A State in Shortage

Spanning across Nebraska, 88 out of 93 counties are designated Mental Health Professional Shortage Areas.

Nearly 95% of Nebraska counties are identified as having a shortage of mental health professionals, and 29 counties have no behavioral health providers at all.

The Human Cost

The impact is real and devastating. From 2016 to 2022, the suicide rate in rural Nebraska was 20.2 per 100,000 residents.

In contrast, the urban rate was 10.0 per 100,000. Rural residents are facing a crisis with half the support.

The Provider Gap

Access is scarce. Rural Nebraska has only 3 practicing psychiatrists per 100,000 residents, compared to 11.8 in urban areas.

Psychologists are also lacking: 8.7 per 100,000 in rural areas versus 27.9 in urban centers.

Distance & Barriers

"Distance is an obstacle because our communities are small and really spread out," says Dr. Cate Jones-Hazledine.

With minimal public transportation, the cost of gas and time off work makes traveling for care a significant burden for isolated communities.

Fighting Stigma

Despite the need, stigma lingers. "A lot of our clinicians are currently really worried about young adult men," notes Dr. Jones-Hazledine.

In small towns, anonymity is hard to find. Some patients park blocks away to avoid being seen at a therapist's office.

A Glimmer of Hope

Progress is happening. Practicing behavioral health providers in rural Nebraska increased by 24% recently.

Telehealth and school-based programs are helping to bridge the gap, bringing care directly to those who need it most.
